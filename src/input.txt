With regard to diplomatic questions, Napoleon summoned Captain Yakovlev,who had been robbed and was in rags and did not know how to get out ofMoscow, minutely explained to him his whole policy and his magnanimity,and having written a letter to the Emperor Alexander in which heconsidered it his duty to inform his Friend and Brother that Rostopchinhad managed affairs badly in Moscow, he dispatched Yakovlev toPetersburg.Having similarly explained his views and his magnanimity to Tutolmin, hedispatched that old man also to Petersburg to negotiate.
